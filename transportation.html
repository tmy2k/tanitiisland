Getting Around Taniti

Whether you arrive by air or by sea, Taniti offers a variety of transportation options to help you explore the island. From small airport arrivals to public buses, taxis, and bike rentals, Taniti's transportation system is designed to make your stay comfortable and convenient. Learn more about the available transport options below.

Air Transportation

Almost all visitors arrive to Taniti by air, though some may arrive on a small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night each week. The island is served by a small airport that accommodates small jets and propeller planes. The airport is in the process of expanding to allow larger jets to land within the next few years.

Ground Transportation

Public buses are available to serve Taniti City and run daily from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. Private buses also serve other parts of the island. Taxis can be hailed in Taniti City, and rental cars are available from a local agency near the airport. For those who want to explore the island more actively, bikes and helmets are available to rent from several vendors (helmets are required by law).

Taniti City is fairly flat and easy to navigate on foot, making it perfect for walking tours. Many tourists choose to stay in the Merriton Landing area, which is easy to explore on foot due to its convenient location and pedestrian-friendly streets.

Rental Cars

If you're planning to explore beyond Taniti City, rental cars are available at a local agency near the airport. Enjoy the flexibility of driving at your own pace and discover hidden gems around the island.

Bike Rentals

Rent a bike to explore the flat and scenic streets of Taniti City. Helmets are required by law, and several local vendors offer rentals for both bikes and helmets.
